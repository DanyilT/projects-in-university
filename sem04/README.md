Operating Systems
Module Title: Operating Systems Module Status: Mandatory
Module Code: COMP H2014 Credit Rating: 5
Module Lecturer: Dr. Kevin Farrell
Module Aims:
This module is a balance between theoretical and practical aspects of modern operating
systems.
The aim of this module is to give learners an appreciation and understanding of the internal workings and
designs of modern operating systems while also providing learners with the necessary basic knowledge,
skills and understanding to install, configure and troubleshoot the Linux operating system. The module is
a balance between theoretical and practical aspects of modern operating systems. The module aims to:
• develop the students’ knowledge of operating systems by covering the theory of how modern,
multi-user, network operating systems function; in particular the issues of concurrency and
deadlock, which arise, in such systems on uni-processor and multi-processor/multicore platforms
• enhance the students’ operating systems administration skills through the advanced configuration
and troubleshooting of the Linux operating systems in a multi-user networked environment.
Indicative Syllabus:
• Operating Systems Structures
• Processes
• Processor Scheduling
• Threads
• Memory Management
• File Management
• I/O Management
• Process Synchronisation
• Deadlocks
• System Administration in Linux
Assessment Procedures:
Examination: 50% Continuous Assessment 50%
Repeat Assessment: 100% Examination

 IT Business Management
Module Title: IT Business Management Module Status: Mandatory
Module Code: H2034 Credit Rating: 5
Module Lecturer: Marie Brennan
Module Aims:
• Identify and explain the basic functions of management and describe the fundamental skills of a
manager
• Examine the planning process and discuss the benefits and limitations of planning.
• Describe the nature and complexity of organisational change and describe the steps in the
change process
• Describe the nature of leadership and evaluate the approaches to leadership and contemporary
IT management practices.
• Communicate the processes of control and describe how control systems support the attainment
of organisational objectives
Indicative Syllabus:
Introduction to IT Management
The Nature of Management; Basic Management Functions; Management Skills; Management
Roles and Responsibilities; The Development of IT Management Thought; Forces on
Organisations; New management Competencies; Managing Crises and Unexpected Events.
IT Management Planning
Organisational Goals and Plans; Components of Strategy; Using SWOT analysis to Formulate
Plans; Types of Plans; Planning in Turbulent Times; The Planning Process; Traditional and
High Performance Approaches to Planning; Perspectives on Decision Making; The Decision
Making Process; Behavioural Aspects of Decision Making; Group Decision Making.
IT Management Organising
Basic Elements of Organising; Basic Forms of Organisation Design; Emerging Issues in
Organisation Design; Factors Shaping Structure; Planned Organisational Change;
Implementing Change; Types of Change; Areas of Organisational Change; Organisational
Innovation.
Leading in an IT environment
The Nature of Leadership; Leadership vs. Management; Approaches to Leadership; Leading
Change; Power and Influence; Leadership for Turbulent Times; Styles of Leadership;
Leadership Behaviours.
IT Management Controlling
The Nature of Control; Operations Control; Methods and Types of Control; Managing Control;
The Control Process; Effective Control; Resistance to Control.
Assessment Procedures Specific to this Module:
Course Work Assessment 100% Continuous Assessment
Repeat Assessment:
Coursework Only
This module is reassessed solely on the basis of re-submitted coursework.
